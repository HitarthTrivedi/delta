# STEP 1 — THE PITCH
## 5 minutes. Pain → Product → Progress → People → Business Model.

*Open with:* "We'd like to take about 5 minutes for the pitch, and then we'd love to go wherever you want from there."

---

### PAIN — The Huge Unsolved Problem
*(~90 seconds)*

Picture a final-year CS student in Vadodara. She has a laptop, decent grades, and a genuine desire to build a career in AI. She opens ChatGPT and asks for a roadmap. It gives her a beautiful 12-step plan. She closes the tab. Next week she opens a new session. ChatGPT has no memory of who she is or what she wants. The plan is gone. She starts over.

This happens to **40 million students in India every year** — and over a billion globally. They're not lazy. They're not unmotivated. They are making the most important decisions of their lives with almost no real information.

They Google "best skills 2026" and get listicles written for nobody. They buy ₹50,000 courses and finish them with no idea whether those skills are rising or dying in the job market right now. They talk to career counselors who haven't looked at a live job posting in a decade. And the ones who hustle hardest often end up with 10 half-built side projects on their GitHub — none of which a recruiter would ever stop at.

The result is a generation that is **busy but not progressing.** Motion without direction. Effort without proof.

Here is the core failure: three things that must be connected are never connected — *who you are*, *what the market wants this week*, and *what you should actually do today.* Every tool on the market solves one corner of that triangle. Nobody closes the full loop.

The global career services, edtech, and upskilling markets total over **$300 billion** — and virtually every rupee of it goes toward content and credentials, not intelligence and direction. The whole market is selling courses to people who don't know which course to take. That is the gap we are filling.

---

### PRODUCT — What Delta Does
*(~90 seconds)*

Delta is an **AI Career Operating System.** Not a course. Not a chatbot. Not a job board. Think of it as a **personal Chief of Staff for your career** — one that never forgets where you started, never stops watching the market, and never lets you drift.

Here is what happens every week, forever, on a loop:

**First**, Delta understands you — through a warm AI intake conversation that adapts to your stage. It works whether you're in 12th grade, mid-career, or switching fields. Upload a resume and it parses every signal from it; then the AI goes deeper — your goals, your constraints, your available time, what success actually looks like to you. The output isn't a transcript. It's structured career intelligence, stored in what we call the **Career Memory Vault**.

**The Vault is the defensible core.** It holds a structured, durable model of you: your skills and their depth, your goals and their priority, your past decisions and why you made them, your constraints — time, money, internet access, family pressure — and your full journey log from day one to today. The longer you use Delta, the smarter it gets and the harder it is to leave. That is a data moat, not a feature.

**Second**, every week Delta reads the real world — job descriptions, rising tools on GitHub, what's trending on StackExchange, which certifications recruiters keep asking for, which hackathons are coming up. It doesn't store links. It stores *meaning* — skills rising, skills dying, proof signals that actually get rewarded.

**Third**, it fuses your context with market reality and produces: a living roadmap with phases and weekly tasks, a project recommendation engineered to be a resume headline rather than a weekend hobby, a portfolio assessment that tells you honestly what's missing, and a **Weekly Brief** — *are you on track? what changed in the market? what changed in you? what to build this week, what to skip.*

Concrete improvements: students build 2 strong projects instead of 10 weak ones. They stop applying blind and start applying ready. And we can prove it because the Vault has logged every week of their journey.

*(~60 seconds)*

**Delta is live and deployed.** Not a concept, not a wireframe — a product on Vercel and Render that real users can sign up for today.

What is shipped and running: adaptive AI onboarding with resume parsing, the Career Memory Vault with semantic memory graph, weekly roadmap generation pulling live market signals from GitHub and job boards, Agent 2 — a persistent AI chat that knows your full plan and can rewrite your weekly task list through natural conversation — an ATS resume engine that exports recruiter-ready `.docx` in one click, Delta Score which gives you an honest weekly grade of where you stand vs. the market, and a full production stack including Google OAuth, rate limiting, Redis caching, and Postgres.

And this is not a one-product team. **Alpha.Kore has shipped 5 live products in under 12 months:** Delta, Lazycook AI (live on PyPI — `pip install lazycook`), Rezum AI, Prism, and 3f1. We don't pitch ideas. We ship things, put them in production, and iterate. That track record is the evidence that what we are describing here already exists and already works.

*(~45 seconds)*

**Freemium** is the acquisition layer. Free intake and a first roadmap hook every student on day one. Within one week the Vault has started building, the first weekly brief has arrived, the resume has been touched. The switching cost grows fast.

**Delta Pro** at ₹299–₹799 per month unlocks the full weekly loop, unlimited Agent 2 chat, portfolio assessment, and ATS resume engine. That is the revenue engine — targeted at the student who opens Delta every Monday and needs it to keep running.

# STEP 2 — THE MAGIC QUESTION
## "What would you like to know more about?"

Every investor walks in with one burning question. Here are the ones we'll face and how to answer each one honestly.

---

### Q: "How many users do you have? What's your traction?"

**Honest answer:** We are pre-scale, not pre-product. The full product is live and functional — every core feature works end-to-end. What we don't yet have is the user growth number that shows the flywheel is spinning. That's exactly what this raise is for: the first 100,000 students through college partnership distribution. We are not raising to build the product. We are raising to put a working product in front of the market.

What we *do* have as validation: the product has been tested end-to-end with real profiles. The weekly loop runs, the roadmap adapts, the resume generates — these aren't mockups. And the studio's track record — 5 live products including a PyPI package with real downloads — proves this team ships things people actually use.

---

### Q: "Why will students pay for this? What's your monetization?"

**Freemium** is the acquisition layer — intake + a first roadmap is free. That's the hook. Delta is designed to become indispensable within the first week: the Vault starts growing, the weekly brief arrives, the resume starts reflecting real progress. Switching cost builds fast.

**Delta Pro** (subscription, target ₹299–₹799/month) unlocks the full weekly loop, unlimited Agent 2 chat, ATS resume engine, and portfolio assessment. That's the revenue engine.

**B2B2C** is the scale play: license Delta to colleges and bootcamps as their career-intelligence layer. One college deal gives us 1,000–10,000 users at once. The institution looks modern; we get distribution and anonymized aggregate data that sharpens our market model for everyone.

**Future:** Once Delta knows who is genuinely job-ready — with *proof* in the Vault — it becomes the most accurate hiring signal in the world. A talent marketplace is a second, larger company hiding inside the first.

---

### Q: "Who are your competitors? What's your edge?"

ChatGPT forgets you. Coursera sells content and walks away. LinkedIn shows you jobs you're not ready for. Career counselors don't scale and don't track the market.

Nobody connects the three things that have to be connected: *who you are*, *what the market wants this week*, and *what you should do now.* Every competitor solves one corner of this triangle. Delta is the only system that closes the full loop.

Our structural moat is the **Career Memory Vault** — a structured, durable model of the person that grows every week. The longer you use Delta, the smarter it gets and the harder it is to leave. That's a data moat, not a feature list. A new entrant can copy the interface in a month. They cannot copy two years of a user's career history, weekly reflections, and market-adjusted journey log.

---

### Q: "What's your biggest technical risk?"

We'll be direct. Right now, our AI infrastructure runs on a **5-key Gemini rotation** to stay under 15 RPM on Google AI Studio's free tier. It works today at low scale. It is not a production-grade reliability story for 100,000 users.

The fix is part of this raise: switch to an **enterprise API with guaranteed quota, SLA, and 100K+ RPM** — transforming our reliability narrative from "we rotate 5 free keys" to "we run on enterprise infrastructure with uptime guarantees." That is not just a technical upgrade; it is a cleaner story for every enterprise and college we pitch to.

Additionally: our **Market Pulse opportunity adapters** currently run in mock mode — the hooks for live job/hackathon data are built, but we haven't yet subscribed to paid data sources. That's funded by this raise too.

---

### Q: "Why India first? Can this work globally?"

India is the sharpest wedge on the planet for this problem: 40M+ students in higher education, graduating into a brutally competitive, opaque job market, where the difference between a good outcome and a bad one is often just *knowing what to build and when.* Cultural fit is strong — Indian students are goal-oriented, metric-driven, and deeply anxious about career direction. They are exactly the user who will open Delta every Monday morning.

Global expansion is structural, not speculative. The product's Domain Pack architecture is not hardcoded to Indian CS students. We extend to Data/AI, Design, Product, Finance, and Research by swapping the domain taxonomy and market sources. The career intelligence problem exists everywhere a motivated person faces an opaque job market — which is everywhere.

---

### Q: "What are the gaps? What are you worried about?"

Here's what's honest:

1. **No paying users yet.** We built the product before we built the funnel. The product is real; the commercial engine is not yet running.
2. **Email delivery is stubbed.** The daily reminder system is architected but Resend is not wired to production. It's a one-day task — but it's not done.
3. **Opportunity adapters are in mock mode.** Live job/internship/hackathon data feeds are built as stubs. We need paid data subscriptions to activate them.
4. **No B2B pipeline yet.** Zero signed college partnerships. The B2B thesis is sound but untested in market.
5. **Two-person team.** At the current velocity we can build; to simultaneously distribute and build we need to add one engineer and one growth person.

The raise addresses all five directly.

---

### Q: "What does ₹2.5 crore actually buy you?"

The real need is ₹2.03 crore. The extra ₹47 lakh is an intentional buffer — AI output costs spike non-linearly as users generate longer roadmaps and more chat history gets passed as context. There will be a month where costs double unexpectedly. That buffer is what keeps us from going back to investors cap-in-hand before the runway ends.

Here's how the ₹2.03Cr is deployed:

| Use | Amount | What it buys |
|-----|--------|--------------|
| **AI Infrastructure** | ₹45L | Switch from 5-key Gemini rotation to enterprise API (100K+ RPM, SLA). Eliminates our biggest reliability risk and transforms our technical narrative. |
| **Real-Time Market Data** | ₹30L | Paid data subscriptions for live job market signals — LinkedIn Talent Insights or equivalent. Activates the mock opportunity adapters and makes Market Pulse a real competitive advantage. |
| **College Distribution** | ₹70L | Partnerships with 10–20 colleges in Year 1. Sales resources, integration support, on-campus activation. This is how we get to 100K students. |
| **Team** | ₹45L | One backend/infra engineer + one growth/partnerships hire. Founders stay focused on product and fundraising; team maintains velocity. |
| **Infrastructure & Ops** | ₹13L | Managed Redis, production Postgres, monitoring (Sentry/Datadog), CDN, compliance setup for institutional deals. |
| **Total Operating Need** | **₹2.03Cr** | 18-month runway to first revenue milestone |
| **Buffer (non-linear AI costs)** | **₹47L** | Hedge against AI cost spikes as roadmap length and chat history scale up |
| **Total Ask** | **₹2.5Cr** | |

# STEP 3 — NEXT STEPS
## Watch the time. Close with a concrete outcome.

---

At 3–4 minutes before the meeting ends:

> *"We're having a great conversation and I'm very conscious of your time. How do you see the next steps from here? Is this something you'd want to explore further?"*

---

### If they say yes:

Get a follow-up meeting on the calendar before leaving the room. Agree on what to prepare — data room, demo walkthrough, a call with a user, or a deeper technical review.

---

### If they say not yet — what they might say and what to do:

**"Come back when you have user numbers."**
→ Set a milestone: 1,000 active users or first paying subscriber. Execute. Email them when it's done with a one-paragraph update. They respect founders who don't give up.

**"You need someone commercial on the team."**
→ This is a real gap. Acknowledge it. Identify whether a business co-founder or a growth hire addresses it. Return with either a named hire or a signed LOI.

**"The AI cost story concerns me at scale."**
→ Walk them through the enterprise API switch and the buffer math. Show them the unit economics model. This is a solvable, funded problem — not a structural flaw.

**"I need to see a signed college deal."**
→ Accelerate the B2B pipeline. One signed MOU with a tier-2 college changes the entire conversation. Prioritize it as the next unlock.

---

Whatever the outcome, follow up within 24 hours with a one-paragraph summary of what you discussed and one specific thing you will have done by the time you reach out again. Keep that promise. Come back.
