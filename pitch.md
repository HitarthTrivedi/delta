## How It Works — The Product

### 🧠 1. Adaptive AI Intake
No forms. A warm, conversational AI advisor interviews the student like a great mentor would — adapting every question to their stage. It works for **everyone**: a 12th-grade student, a college dropout, an undergrad, a master's candidate, a career-switcher, someone with no resume at all.

Upload a resume and a **high-accuracy parsing engine** extracts every skill, project, and signal in one shot. Then the AI does what a resume *can't* — it has a real conversation about your **goals, ambitions, motivation, and what success actually looks like to you.** The output isn't a transcript; it's **structured career intelligence.**

### 🗄️ 2. The Career Memory Vault
The defensible core. Not one blob of chat — a structured, durable model of the person:

| Profile | What it holds |
|---|---|
| **Identity** | Stage, background, location, language |
| **Ambition** | Dream roles, target industries, long & short-term goals |
| **Capability** | Skills, depth, proof, confidence, gaps |
| **Behavior** | Consistency, study habits, learning velocity |
| **Constraints** | Time, money, devices, family, internet — the *real* limits |
| **Preferences** | Learning style, content taste, communication tone |
| **Evidence** | Projects, certs, GitHub, contests, portfolio |
| **Journey Log** | Day-one-to-today career history |

Every feature reads from the Vault *before* it acts. **The longer you use Delta, the smarter it gets and the harder it is to leave.** This is the data moat.

### 📡 3. Market Pulse
Every week, Delta scans the real world — job descriptions, internships, hackathons, Kaggle, LeetCode/Codeforces trends, Unstop competitions, GitHub momentum, certification demand, and global AI-tooling shifts. It doesn't store links. It stores **meaning**: skills rising, skills dying, tools recruiters keep mentioning, the proof signals that actually get rewarded, and the deadlines you can't miss.

Your roadmap only changes *after* market reality meets your personal context. **No other tool does this fusion.**

### 🗺️ 4. The Living Roadmap
Not a checklist — a **career map** with two zoom levels. Zoom out: phases, milestones, destination, role-readiness. Zoom in: this week, today's tasks, your current blocker, the next proof to build. Each phase explains *why it exists, what to learn, what proof to create, which resources fit your style, and what a strong candidate looks like after it.* When you skip, overperform, or discover a new interest — **the map redraws itself, and logs why.**

### 🔁 5. The Weekly Loop
The heartbeat. Every week Delta reads your memory, reads last week's journey, fetches the market pulse, compares you against what the market expects, updates the roadmap, generates your plan and calendar, surfaces opportunities, recommends one meaningful project, assesses your portfolio weight, and writes you a **Weekly Brief**: *Are you on track? What changed in the world? What changed in you? What to build, what to avoid, what to apply to.*

### 🏗️ 6. Projects as Proof & Portfolio Assessment
Delta recommends **fewer, stronger projects** engineered to be resume headlines — with the real-world problem, required skills, milestones, evaluation criteria, README and demo expectations, and the recruiter-facing pitch. Then it grades your portfolio honestly: what proof you have, what's missing, what looks weak, what to fix *before* you apply. The goal is **career weight, not cosmetic completion.**

### 📄 7. The ATS Resume Engine
A live resume that **generates itself from your Delta profile**, optimizes bullet points against real market keywords (ATS mode), proposes bi-weekly updates as you grow, and exports recruiter-ready `.docx` in one click. Your resume stops being a document you dread and becomes a **dashboard of your progress.**

### 💬 8. Always-On Career Chat
A second AI agent with **full memory of your plan** — ask it anything, and it answers in the context of your goals, this week's tasks, and your history. It remembers the conversation across weeks. It is the mentor that is always awake.

### 🌐 9. Domain Packs — Beyond CS
The architecture is **not hardcoded to engineering.** Domain packs extend Delta to Data/AI, Design, Product, Finance, Core Engineering, Research, and Entrepreneurship — each with its own skill taxonomy, market sources, proof types, projects, and rubrics. **Start with Indian CS students; expand to every ambitious person on Earth.**

## Why We Win — The Architecture Is the Moat

Delta runs a **dual-model engine** tuned for both quality and cost: the rare, accuracy-critical work (parsing your life) runs on a frontier model; the high-frequency conversation runs on an efficient model. **We get frontier-quality intelligence at a fraction of frontier cost** — a structural margin advantage that compounds as we scale.

And the system is built on a hard set of **non-negotiables** most "AI career tools" violate every day:
- Never store understanding as raw chat — always structure it.
- Never generate a roadmap without market context.
- Never give weekly advice without reading the journey.
- Never recommend a resource without explaining why it fits.
- Never recommend a project unless it creates visible proof.
- **Never pretend the user is progressing if the evidence says they're not.**

Delta is honest, adaptive, and deeply personal — by design, in code.
